# Cognitive Biases, the Default Mode Network, and Psychedelics

---

## The Filtering Brain

If human beings were consciously aware of all the data points they're faced with on a normal day, they'd go crazy. This is the reason our brain develops many of our biases, they are compression tools.

Hypothesis: some mental illnesses may result from the bias-brain breaking down and letting in too much stimulation. Autistic sensory overload as evidence.

"The big problem with human beings is we think we're the only exception."

---

## Key Biases

**Illusion of objectivity**, individuals believe they are less biased than others.

**Better-than-average effect**, most people consider themselves above average in intelligence, kindness, and various qualities. Logically impossible for most to be true simultaneously.

Both reflect a broader tendency to see ourselves as unique or exceptional, which distorts self-perception and how we relate to others.

---

## Dissolution of Cognitive Biases via Psychedelics

Psychedelics disrupt the usual cognitive biases and mental shortcuts the brain employs to simplify the world. They diminish the influence of the **default mode network (DMN)**, the brain network associated with self-referential thought and ego.

The DMN is also linked to the maintenance of cognitive biases such as confirmation bias and self-serving bias. By reducing DMN activity, psychedelics can temporarily lessen the grip of these biases, potentially allowing for more objective or novel perspectives.
